Improv Games 2

Consciences - two actors act a scene while two off stage reveal what they are really thinking.
Gibberish expert - one actor is an expert in subject chosen by the audience but speaks only in gibberish, the other actor translates.
Dubbing - two actors act a movie scene in a foreign language, two others translate.
Superheroes - the audience choose a superpower and a threat the superheroes need to tackle. Each actor calls in another hero with a superb power of their choice to help them deal with the problem.
Chain murder - three actors leave the room, while the audience chose the room and weapon in a murder. Each actor then calls in the other and acts the scene without describing the room or weapon. The last actor has to say what he thought was the room and weapon.
Peapod to frying pan - the audience chose a starting and an ending scene and two actors have to get from one to the other. They are told to change scenes at points when they have assumed interesting positions.
Alphabet game - the audience choose the starting letter and profession and two actors carry out a scene each starting with a consecutive letter.
Song - the audience choose a situation and the actors start making related sounds. Once a backing rhythm is established one or two of the actors can start singing.
He said, she said - two actors act a scene chosen by the audience. After each line the other actor says "s/he said doing XYZ", allowing each actor to get the other in trouble.
No 's' - two actors have to act a scene without using any words containing the letter 's'.
Death in a minute - the actors have to die in as many interesting ways as possible in a minute.
