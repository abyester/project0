Learning about Meisner

"Act before you think - your instincts are more honest than your thoughts."

Quote from Sanford Meisner on Acting

Repetition Stage Details
Basic repetition Say something truthful about the other person and then that person repeats from their perspective. Start with something basic like "you're wearing a blue shirt" to which they reply "I'm wearing a blue shirt". Continue to repeat what you hear until something happens that makes you change.
More advanced repetition Thereâ€™s no need to change what you say or how you say it unless you see something new occurring. Simply put: if you see the person fidgeting and biting their lip, you may believe they are nervous, then say it and continue to repeat (until one of you sees some new change occuring).
Rinse and repeat There are three rules for repetition:
  1. Tell the Truth
  2. If in doubt Repeat
  3. Dont stop playing the game
Keep playing if you get it right, get it wrong or completely mess it up. Place your focus on your partner and play until told to stop. Say what you see regardless of politeness - to be able to act truthfully in a wide range of imaginary circumstance you must be unrestricted by social niceties.
